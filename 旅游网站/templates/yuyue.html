Stop - over Flight

price: $300

A stop - over flight refers to a flight in which the aircraft needs to land at an intermediate airport during the flight and stay for a period of time before continuing to the destination. There are various reasons for the stop, such as refueling, boarding and disembarking some passengers, etc. For example, a flight from City A to City C makes a stop - over in City B. In City B, some passengers may get off the plane, and new passengers may board the plane, and then the flight takes off again and heads for City C. For airlines, this type of flight can optimize the route layout and improve the utilization rate of the aircraft; for passengers, sometimes they may have the opportunity to experience more airport facilities or take a short rest at the stop - over place, but it may also increase the travel time due to the stop - over.

Code - sharing Flight

price: $300

Code - sharing is a form of cooperation between airlines. Simply put, one airline (the marketing airline) can use the flights of another airline (the operating airline) and sell them under its own flight number. For example, if Airline A and Airline B reach a code - sharing agreement, Airline A may regard a certain flight operated by Airline B as its own flight in its flight reservation system. Passengers purchase tickets from Airline A, but actually take the flight operated by Airline B. This way can enable airlines to expand their route networks, provide passengers with more travel options, and at the same time improve the operational efficiency and resource utilization efficiency of airlines.
